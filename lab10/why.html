Why is PNG Important?

What is PNG? Why PNG? Using PNG Sources

Compression's Impact on Choice

The PNG format is important as it represents one of many web standard tools that can be used to improve both accessibility and presentation. Going back to the concept of compression, it may be hard to understand why somebody wouldn't use PNG if the seeming benefits are so good (consistent quality, colors, etc). The truth is, it isn't that simple, especially for those whose job it is to make websites look good without sacrificing quality or accessibility of content. PNG may not be ideal for every single situation where a designer wants an image, but it has clear benefits over its seemingly identical sibling formats (such as JPEG), which helps give designers more control over their sites. As mentioned before, PNG files may not suffer from quality loss with compression, but it does lead to larger file sizes. That is an example of something to think about when developing a site, even if most others would ignore it. Additionally, PNG can be quite useful for more simple yet detailed web graphics.

Transparency

PNG files have another major difference, which is how they can handle transparency in images. They contain multiple levels of transparency, unlike JPEG and GIF (GIF allows for one color to be configured as transparent). These multiple levels of transparency can go a long way in making a site look flashier, with interesting web graphic designs. This is especially important and useful for the more creative web designers (with pages much prettier than this), as they can use this transparency advantage to create some truly complex and incredible designs.
